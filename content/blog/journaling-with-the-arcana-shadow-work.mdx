Shadow work is the practice of exploring the hidden parts of ourselves—the traits, emotions, and desires that we often push into the unconscious. While it can be daunting, shadow work is the most direct path to authentic self-love and spiritual integration. 

Tarot provides the perfect structure for this exploration. The archetypes of the Major Arcana act as mirrors, reflecting parts of our psyche that we might otherwise ignore. Here is a **7-day journaling challenge** to help you dive deep.

## How to use this challenge

Each day, find a quiet space, light a candle, and have your journal ready. You don’t need to pull the card from your deck; the archetype itself is the prompt. 

### Day 1: The High Priestess (Intuition vs. Secrets)
*What am I hiding from myself? What "knowing" have I been suppressing because it feels inconvenient or scary?*
The High Priestess reminds us that truth often whispers. Compare this with our deeper dive into [The High Priestess and her secrets](/blog/the-high-priestess-secrets).

### Day 2: The Lovers (Choices and Values)
*Where am I making choices based on what I "should" do rather than what I value? In what area of my life am I feeling divided?*

### Day 3: The Hermit (Solitude vs. Loneliness)
*What part of me is afraid of being alone? How can I transform my isolation into intentional, nourishing solitude?* 
Revisit [The Hermit's lesson on the inner voice](/blog/the-hermit-card-how-to-find-your-inner-voice) for inspiration.

### Day 4: Strength (The Shadow Beast)
*What is the "beast" inside me that I keep trying to cage? (Anger, jealousy, ambition?) How can I bring it a little more compassion today?*

### Day 5: The Devil (Attachments and Ego)
*What habit, relationship, or belief system do I feel "chained" to? What would happen if I realized the chains were loose?*

### Day 6: The Tower (Sudden Change)
*What structure in my life—mental or physical—is crumbling? Instead of trying to hold it up, how can I let it fall to make room for the new?* 
For love-specific Tower work, see our guide on [The Tower card in love readings](/blog/the-meaning-of-the-tower-card-in-love-readings).

### Day 7: The Sun (Integration)
*What is my greatest strength? How can I let it shine without feeling like I need to hide or apologize for my success?*
Be mindful of the [shadow side of success](/blog/the-shadow-side-of-the-sun-warning-signs) as you journal.

## Beyond the 7 Days

Shadow work isn't a one-time event; it's a lifelong conversation with yourself. By using tarot as your guide, you make the process less about fear and more about curiosity.

This 7-day challenge is a powerful way to integrate [tarot into your self-care rituals](/blog/using-tarot-for-self-care-ritual). If you find that the shadow work brings up more questions than answers, a [deep-dive reading](/services) can help you find the clarity you need to move forward.
